UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud usually happens through fake calls, messages, or payment requests.

In a typical scenario, a college student receives a call from someone pretending to be a bank representative or a buyer from an online marketplace. The scammer claims that money will be transferred to the student’s account and asks them to “approve” a request or enter their UPI PIN. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment from the victim’s account. Sometimes, victims receive a “collect request” on apps like Google Pay or PhonePe and are tricked into approving it.

Students and first-time digital payment users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI works. The consequences include immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering the money. In some cases, victims may also share personal information, increasing the risk of identity theft.

This type of fraud is increasing due to the rapid adoption of digital payments. Awareness and careful verification are essential to prevent falling victim to such scams.
